SAR Support
Downed Warthog
Indian Ocean

This is a field-ready TRU brief for a search-and-rescue support scenario involving a downed A-10 "Warthog" aircraft in the Indian Ocean. It is written to stay local, factual, and usable when connectivity is poor or absent. No cloud dependency. No guesswork. No hidden authority.

Mission

Support the location, coordination, and recovery effort for the crew of a downed Warthog aircraft over the Indian Ocean. Keep the brief focused on the safety of the crew, the last known position, the rescue authority, and the next concrete action.

Situation

  • Aircraft status is unknown until confirmed.
  • Last known position and time are the first anchor points.
  • Weather, sea state, daylight, and drift matter.
  • Do not assume coordinates, survivability, or comms status.

Task

  • Assemble the cleanest possible picture from known facts.
  • Identify the search datum and keep it updated.
  • Report gaps immediately instead of filling them with confidence.

Constraints

  • Time, fuel, weather, and comms are limiting factors.
  • Authority and jurisdiction must be explicit.
  • Survivor safety outranks narrative clarity.
  • Use only grounded information.

Comms

  • Use short, structured updates.
  • State what is known, what is missing, and what changed.
  • Do not bury the lead.
  • Prefer plain language over jargon.

Decision

The key decision is whether the available facts justify expanding the search area, narrowing it, or holding until better information arrives. If the datum is weak, say so plainly.

First pass checklist

  1. Confirm last known position, time, and source of that fix.
  2. Confirm number of crew and any emergency beacon or mayday reports.
  3. Confirm weather, sea state, and visibility at the time of loss.
  4. Confirm available assets and who is coordinating them.
  5. Confirm the reporting channel and cadence.
  6. Set the search datum and note every assumption separately.

What TRU should never do here

It should not invent coordinates, pretend the crew is recovered, or disguise uncertainty. If the system does not know, it should say exactly what is missing and what the safest next step is.

A1 prompt for the field

A1: SAR support for a downed Warthog aircraft in the Indian Ocean.
Mission: support rescue and recovery of the crew.
Situation: state last known position, time, weather, sea state, daylight, and comms status.
Task: build the search picture and identify the next action.
Constraints: time, fuel, authority, jurisdiction, risk, and missing data.
Comms: use short structured updates.
Decision: expand, narrow, or hold the search area.
Execution: act only on grounded facts.
Report: return a concise SITREP with gaps called out.
Standard: local, factual, concise, and honest.

TRU answer format

Mission → Situation → Task → Constraints → Comms → Decision → Execution → Report.

If facts are missing, TRU should name the gap and stop there. That is the honest answer.
